Custodes Passionis

In the 1300's, the poor nuns of San Silvestro established a church to serve as a bastion of their work: to serve God and humanity through charitable deeds and prayer. Time was less than favorable to their mission. The 19th century brought Napolean and the suppressive religious orders of the Kingdom of Italy, which ultimately converted it to barracks. This sealed its fate durring World War 2 when it was hit by a stray British bomb.

The building was left to rot. Ultimately the roof collapsed, causing the destruction of invaluable frescos and artifacts from the 14th and 15th centuries. The citizens seized it from Genoa's government in an act to save it. This group of individuals, branded as anarchists, kept it alive. It continues to serve the community as a cultural hub where people can self educate and share their work with the city. This architecture is crafted to tell the stories of old and new.
